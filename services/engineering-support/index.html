Engineering Support

Practical guidance through technical and municipal requirements.

Pascoe Engineering supports property owners, developers, and consultant teams with engineering review, municipal coordination, technical advice, and design support throughout the approval process.

How We Help

Engineering support when the next step is not always clear.

Development and property projects often involve technical comments, multiple consultants, municipal requirements, and changing design information.

Pascoe Engineering helps clients understand what is required, review available information, identify technical concerns, and coordinate practical next steps.

Support can be provided as part of a larger civil engineering assignment or as focused advice for a specific issue, submission, or stage of the approval process.

Typical Situations

When focused engineering support can help move a project forward.

Not every assignment begins with a clearly defined engineering scope. Sometimes the first step is understanding the issue, available information, and approval requirements.

Municipal Comments

Understanding and responding to technical review comments.

Review of municipal comments to identify the underlying issue, required revisions, and the consultants or information needed to respond.

Early Project Planning

Identifying likely engineering requirements before design begins.

Early review can help clarify servicing, drainage, grading, survey, reporting, and approval needs before the project team advances too far.

Consultant Coordination

Helping multiple disciplines work toward a coordinated submission.

Civil engineering requirements often need to align with architecture, planning, landscape design, surveying, traffic, and other technical disciplines.

Design Review

Reviewing proposed concepts before formal submission.

A focused review can help identify grading, drainage, servicing, or coordination concerns before they become larger approval issues.

Existing Project Challenges

Practical advice when a project has stalled or changed direction.

Support may include reviewing prior submissions, municipal correspondence, existing drawings, unresolved comments, and practical options for moving forward.

Due Diligence

Early engineering review before committing to a project.

Preliminary review of servicing, grading, drainage, infrastructure, and approval considerations can help identify potential constraints.

Available Services

Focused engineering support tailored to the project stage.

The scope can range from a short technical review to ongoing coordination throughout a development application.

Development Application Support

Engineering assistance throughout planning applications, technical submissions, comment responses, revisions, and municipal review.

Municipal Coordination

Communication with municipal staff and reviewing agencies to clarify requirements, technical concerns, and submission expectations.

Engineering Review

Review of drawings, reports, servicing concepts, grading strategies, drainage concerns, and available background information.

Design Coordination

Coordination between civil engineering requirements and the work of architects, planners, surveyors, landscape architects, and other consultants.

Technical Advice

Clear, practical guidance on engineering issues, submission requirements, municipal comments, and potential next steps.

Due Diligence Review

Preliminary review of infrastructure, drainage, grading, servicing, and approval considerations for a property or proposed development.

Collaborative Support

A civil engineering perspective for the broader project team.

Good coordination helps prevent technical requirements from being addressed too late.

Civil engineering decisions can affect building elevations, site layout, parking, landscaping, utility locations, access, stormwater, and municipal approvals.

Pascoe Engineering works with owners, developers, architects, planners, builders, surveyors, landscape architects, and other professionals to help identify conflicts and maintain a coordinated approach.

What to Expect

A focused process built around the issue or project stage.

Understand

Review the project, issue, and available information.

We begin by understanding the property, approval status, technical concerns, and decisions that need to be made.

Assess

Identify the key engineering and coordination requirements.

Existing drawings, reports, comments, surveys, and project constraints are reviewed to determine the practical next step.

Advise

Provide clear recommendations and technical guidance.

Advice is tailored to the project stage, available information, municipal requirements, and realistic implementation options.

Coordinate

Support communication, revisions, and follow-through.

Where required, we help coordinate with the consultant team, municipality, or reviewing agencies as the project moves forward.
