Land Development

Practical civil engineering for small land development projects.

Pascoe Engineering supports residential, commercial, mixed-use, institutional, and industrial development projects with coordinated grading, servicing, stormwater management, technical reporting, and municipal approval support.

How We Help

Coordinated engineering support from early planning through approval.

Land development projects require multiple engineering elements to work together.

Grading, servicing, stormwater management, access, landscaping, and municipal requirements can all influence the design. Reviewing these elements together helps identify conflicts early and supports a more complete submission.

Pascoe Engineering works with clients and consultant teams to develop practical civil engineering solutions, prepare required drawings and reports, and coordinate technical requirements throughout the municipal approval process.

Typical Projects

Civil engineering support for a range of smaller developments.

The required engineering scope will depend on the property, proposed use, available infrastructure, approval process, and municipal requirements.

Residential Development

Engineering design for new residential communities and infill.

Support for subdivisions, townhouses, condominium developments, custom-home projects, severances, and residential redevelopment.

Commercial Development

Civil design for small commercial properties and expansions.

Grading, servicing, stormwater, and municipal coordination for plazas, offices, retail properties, and commercial additions.

Mixed-Use Development

Coordinated engineering for sites with multiple land uses.

Integrated grading and servicing design supporting residential, commercial, and shared site functions within one development.

Industrial Development

Site engineering for small industrial and employment projects.

Civil design supporting industrial buildings, site expansions, loading areas, parking, servicing, and stormwater controls.

Institutional Projects

Engineering support for community-serving properties.

Grading, drainage, and servicing design for childcare facilities, places of worship, community buildings, and similar uses.

Redevelopment and Infill

Practical design for constrained and previously developed sites.

Review of existing infrastructure, drainage conditions, servicing limitations, and opportunities to support redevelopment.

Available Services

Civil engineering drawings, reports, and approval support.

Services can be provided individually or coordinated as part of a broader civil engineering submission.

Grading Plans

Detailed design of site elevations, slopes, drainage routes, retaining requirements, and connections to surrounding lands.

Servicing Plans

Design of water, sanitary, and storm servicing required to support a proposed development and connect to available infrastructure.

Functional Servicing Reports

Technical reports evaluating how a proposed development can be supported by water, wastewater, stormwater, and drainage infrastructure.

Stormwater Management Reports

Analysis and design addressing stormwater quantity, quality, runoff controls, drainage, and safe overland flow.

Erosion and Sediment Control Plans

Temporary construction measures intended to control erosion and prevent sediment from leaving the development site.

Development Application Support

Engineering assistance for planning applications, technical submissions, municipal comments, revisions, and consultant coordination.

Municipal Engineering Coordination

Coordination with municipal staff and reviewing agencies to help clarify technical requirements and move submissions forward.

Coordinated Design

Civil engineering does not happen in isolation.

A successful development submission depends on coordination between multiple disciplines.

Civil design may need to respond to building elevations, site layout, landscape design, road access, fire routes, utility requirements, environmental constraints, and municipal standards.

Pascoe Engineering works collaboratively with architects, planners, surveyors, landscape architects, traffic consultants, environmental specialists, and other project professionals to help maintain a coordinated design.
