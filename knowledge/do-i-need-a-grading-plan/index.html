Do I Need a Grading Plan?

Understanding when a grading plan may be required for your project and why municipalities ask for one.

Quick Answer

You may need a grading plan if your project changes how water drains across your property or if your municipality requires one as part of a permit application. Common examples include pool installations, additions, retaining walls, and significant landscaping. A civil engineer can help determine whether a grading plan is required based on your project and local requirements.

Why This Question Comes Up

Why would a relatively small project need an engineering plan?

If you have applied for a building permit, spoken with your municipality, or started planning a backyard project, there is a good chance you have heard the term “grading plan.”

For many homeowners, it is not immediately obvious why one is needed. After all, you may be installing a pool or building an addition, not redesigning the entire property.

Even relatively small changes can affect how water drains across a property. A grading plan helps show that the proposed work will not create drainage issues for your property, neighbouring properties, or municipal infrastructure.

Good to Know

A grading plan is not intended to make a project more complicated. Its purpose is to help ensure rainwater drains safely after construction and to reduce the risk of future drainage problems.

The Basics

What is a grading plan?

A grading plan is an engineering drawing that shows the existing and proposed elevations of a property. It illustrates how rainwater will drain after construction and helps confirm that changes to the property will not create drainage issues.

Depending on the project, the plan may show proposed grades, drainage directions, swales, retaining walls, catch basins, and other features that influence how water moves across the site.

When It May Be Needed

When might a grading plan be required?

A grading plan may be required when a project has the potential to change how water drains across a property.

  • Installing a swimming pool
  • Building a home addition
  • Constructing a new custom home
  • Changing the elevation of a yard
  • Building retaining walls
  • Regrading a property
  • Altering an existing drainage pattern
  • Responding to a municipal permit-review requirement

Requirements vary by municipality and project scope. Two projects that appear similar may not have the same submission requirements, so it is best to confirm before construction begins.

Common Situations

Projects that often lead to grading questions

  • An in-ground swimming pool is being installed.
  • A significant home addition changes roof and surface drainage.
  • A municipality identifies drainage concerns during permit review.
  • The slope of a yard is being changed.
  • A retaining wall affects drainage near a property line.
  • New driveways, patios, or other hard surfaces increase runoff.

Common Questions

Questions homeowners often ask

Does every pool require a grading plan?

Not necessarily. Requirements depend on the municipality, the property, and the scope of work. It is best to confirm before preparing a permit submission.

Who prepares a grading plan?

Grading plans are commonly prepared by a civil engineer with experience in site grading, drainage, and municipal approval requirements.

Can my contractor prepare the plan?

A contractor can provide useful construction information, but a municipality may require the plan to be prepared or reviewed by a qualified professional.

How long does it take?

Timing depends on the project, the information available, and whether a survey or other background material is required.
