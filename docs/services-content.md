# Service Category 1: Homeowner Projects

## Category Introduction

Engineering support for residential property improvements, permit applications, and drainage concerns.

### Pool Grading Plans

Grading plans for new swimming pools, pool removals, and associated backyard improvements. The plan demonstrates how surface drainage will be managed while protecting neighbouring properties and existing drainage patterns.

Typical projects:

- New swimming pools
- Pool removals
- Backyard landscaping associated with a pool
- Pool permit applications
- Drainage improvements around an existing pool

### Lot Grading Plans

Grading plans that establish proposed elevations, slopes, drainage directions, and property-line relationships for residential lots.

Typical projects:

- New homes
- Residential additions
- Infill development
- Major landscaping projects
- Permit and municipal submissions

### Drainage Plans

Site-specific reviews and plans addressing drainage concerns, proposed improvements, and the movement of surface water across a residential property.

Typical projects:

- Backyard drainage concerns
- Water flowing toward a building
- Impacts on neighbouring properties
- Regrading and landscaping
- Drainage improvements associated with construction

### Driveway Grading Plans

Engineering plans supporting new or modified driveways while considering property drainage, boulevard grades, garage elevations, and municipal requirements.

Typical projects:

- Driveway widening
- Driveway relocation
- New driveway entrances
- Garage additions
- Changes to existing property access

# Service Category 2: Residential Development

## Category Introduction

Engineering design and technical reporting supporting small residential developments and municipal approval applications.

### Grading Plans

Detailed grading design establishing site elevations, slopes, overland drainage routes, retaining requirements, and connections to surrounding lands.

Typical projects:

- Small subdivisions
- Infill developments
- Townhouse projects
- Severed residential lots
- Redevelopment sites

### Servicing Plans

Design of water, sanitary, and storm servicing required to support a proposed development.

Typical projects:

- New residential developments
- Infill and intensification projects
- Service connections
- Internal site servicing
- Municipal servicing extensions

### Functional Servicing Reports

Engineering reports evaluating how a proposed development will be serviced by municipal water, sanitary, and stormwater infrastructure.

Typical projects:

- Official Plan and Zoning By-law Amendments
- Draft Plans of Subdivision
- Site Plan Applications
- Consent and severance applications
- Development feasibility reviews

### Stormwater Management Reports

Technical analysis and design addressing stormwater quantity, quality, runoff controls, and safe drainage for a proposed development.

Typical projects:

- New residential development
- Redevelopment and intensification
- Increased impervious area
- Municipal approval applications
- Sites requiring on-site stormwater controls

### Erosion and Sediment Control Plans

Plans identifying temporary measures used to control erosion and prevent sediment from leaving a construction site.

Typical projects:

- Site clearing and grading
- Residential construction
- Development servicing
- Municipal permit requirements
- Sites near roads, sewers, or natural features

# Service Category 3: Engineering Support

## Category Introduction

Professional guidance to help clients understand technical requirements and move projects through the municipal approval process.

### Development Application Support

Engineering assistance throughout the planning and development application process, including coordination of required studies, drawings, and technical submissions.

### Municipal Coordination

Communication and coordination with municipal staff, reviewing agencies, planners, architects, surveyors, and other project consultants.

### Engineering Review

Review of existing drawings, reports, servicing concepts, drainage concerns, and technical requirements to identify issues and practical next steps.

### Design Coordination

Coordination between civil engineering design and the work of architects, planners, landscape architects, surveyors, and other professionals.

# Our Process

## 1. Initial Discussion

We learn about the property, project objectives, approval status, and the challenges that need to be addressed.

## 2. Scope and Information Review

We identify the required engineering services, available background information, municipal requirements, and any additional information that may be needed.

## 3. Engineering Design

We prepare the required drawings, calculations, reports, and supporting documentation.

## 4. Submission and Coordination

We support the submission process, respond to technical comments, and coordinate revisions where required.
